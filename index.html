📋 Section A — Taking a History
1
Employment & Self-Certification ▼
  • Are they currently employed?
  • What is the role? Manual / office / client-facing?
  • Have they already self-certified for 7 days?
  • If unemployed — is a FIT note clinically indicated?
2
Nature of the Job ▼
  • What do they actually do day-to-day?
  • Cognitive and physical demands of the role?
  • Could any duties be modified or reduced?
  • Is home working possible?
3
Health Beliefs ▼
  • Do they feel they must be 100% before returning?
  • What are their expectations around recovery?
  • Do symptoms resolve when on holiday? (suggests work as a driver)
  • Previous difficulty returning to work?
4
Other Responsibilities ▼
  • Caring responsibilities at home?
  • Are these affecting capacity to work or recover?
  • Financial stressors driving the request?
5
Work History ▼
  • Previous time off — was returning to work difficult?
  • How did previous absences resolve?
  • Do symptoms improve significantly on holiday?
✅ Section B — The Consultation
1
Functional Impact ▼

Ask: "What is it about your condition that stops you working right now?"

Document specific limitations — not just the diagnosis.

✅ "Unable to concentrate >20 mins. Cannot face customer contact."

❌ "Anxiety — not fit for work"

2
May be Fit for Work? ▼

Consider actively before defaulting to 'not fit for work':

  • Phased return (e.g. 3 days/week initially)
  • Altered hours (e.g. later start, avoid rush hour)
  • Amended duties (e.g. less patient-facing work)
  • Workplace adaptations (quiet space, WFH days)
If 'not fit' — document why adjustments aren't appropriate.
3
Duration & Review ▼
  • First certificate: typically no more than 3 months
  • Repeat notes: reassess functionally — do not copy previous note
  • Ask: "What needs to change for you to feel ready to return?"
  • Consider F2F appointment for repeat notes
  • Set a defined review date — no open-ended notes
4
Signpost ▼

Offer at least one relevant resource every time. Document what was offered in the clinical record. See signposting section below.

5
Plan the Follow-Up ▼

Frame as a partnership:

"Let's review in 4 weeks — the aim is to think about what a phased return might look like."

Ask reception to book follow-up automatically when a mental health FIT note is printed.

💬 Helpful Discussion Points — tap to expand ▼

Work is usually beneficial for physical and mental health in the long run. The risk of not returning increases the longer you are off.

It is not expected that you need to be 100% before you return to work.

We can issue a note with an end date and review you to consider returning to work with a 'May be Fit' option.

With these adjustments, we hope to help your employer understand what support might help facilitate you going back to work.

Our job is not just to sign you off — it's also to help you figure out how to get back.
